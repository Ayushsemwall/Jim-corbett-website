Pakhro Zone: A Gateway to Wilderness

The Pakhro Zone serves as a significant entry point to the expansive wilderness of Jim Corbett National Park, accessible from near Kotdwar in the Pauri Garhwal district of Uttarakhand. As a gateway to the Sonanadi Range, Pakhro offers a distinct and less-trodden path into the wild. This zone is celebrated for its raw, untamed beauty and provides a more tranquil safari experience, ideal for those seeking a serene connection with nature.

🌳 Pakhro Sanctuary Highlights

  • Wildlife: Spot majestic tigers, elusive leopards, herds of elephants, sloth bears, sambar and barking deer, and wild boars.
  • Birds: Paradise for birdwatchers with hornbills, kingfishers, eagles, and other woodland and water birds.
  • Natural Beauty: Dense Sal forests, undulating terrain, and pristine riverbeds offering scenic views.
  • Safari Experience: Limited safari jeeps ensure peaceful encounters with wildlife and higher chances of sightings.

🏡 Forest Rest House in Pakhro

Stay at the historic Pakhro Forest Rest House for an immersive jungle experience. Rustic rooms provide comfort amidst dense forest, with proximity to safari gates for early morning and evening excursions. Enjoy the nocturnal sounds of the jungle and fully disconnect from the outside world.

📅 Plan Your Visit

  • Best Time: November to June for pleasant weather and frequent wildlife sightings.
  • Safari Timings: Morning (6:00 AM – 9:30 AM) and Evening (2:30 PM – 6:00 PM).
  • Guided Safaris: Book via official park website or authorized operators; max 6 visitors per jeep with a guide.
  • Nearby Attractions: Proximity to Sonanadi Zone and Kotdwar town.
  • Tips: Wear earthy tones, carry binoculars and a camera, and follow park rules for safety.

🌿 The Pakhro Zone offers a refreshing and authentic wildlife experience, combining rich biodiversity with a serene atmosphere.
