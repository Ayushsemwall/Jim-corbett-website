About the Park

Established in 1936, Jim Corbett National Park is Asia's first national park and one of the most famous tiger reserves in India.

Overview of Jim Corbett

Jim Corbett (Edward James Corbett, 1875–1955) was a legendary hunter, tracker, naturalist, and conservationist whose life inspired India’s first national park.

Geography

Located in Nainital and Pauri Garhwal districts, spanning over 1,300 km² at the Himalayan foothills.

Flora & Fauna

Royal Bengal Tiger, Asiatic Elephant, Leopard, Sloth Bear, Deer species, 600+ bird species.

Ecotourism

Visitors enjoy jeep/canter safaris, bird watching, nature walks, and conservation programs.
